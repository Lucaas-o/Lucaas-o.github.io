Hosting Architecture Guide

Keep your bot online 24/7 with these streamlined deployment steps.

Your bot is a powerful tool, but it needs an environment to live in. Whether you use an Android device or a dedicated machine, this guide ensures your community never loses its silicon companion.

01

Environment Setup

We'll use Termux for Android or a Node.js environment for PC. Termux is a free, powerful terminal emulator that acts like a mini-server on your phone.

Download Termux (v0.118+)
02

Initialization Commands

Execute these commands in sequence to prepare your environment.

Terminal
pkg update && pkg upgrade
pkg install nodejs
pkg install unzip
03

Deploying Bot Files

Upload your bot's identity files. I'll provide these in a secure ZIP package.

  1. Move the ZIP to your device's home directory.
  2. Use unzip YourBot.zip to extract the core architecture.
  3. Navigate to the root: cd YourBot
04

Ignition

Start the process and bring your bot to life.

Terminal
node index.js

Once you see "Bot is Online!", you're live!

Pro-Tips for 24/7 Uptime

  • Power Management: Keep the device connected to a stable power source.
  • Connectivity: Ensure background data usage is allowed for Termux.
  • Recovery: If the device restarts, simply re-run the node index.js command.
